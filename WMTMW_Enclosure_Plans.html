Bracing Layout — F-B Ties & Back Battens

F-B ties — 6 total, all three chambers: 3/4" × 3/4" × 4.50" MDF blocks bridging the baffle back face to the back panel front face. Two per chamber, one on each side of the driver(s). Use a cardboard template of the baffle cutouts to confirm placement clears every cutout by ≥1" before gluing.

Back battens — 2 total, woofer chambers only: 3/4" × 3/4" × 13.25" MDF strips glued flat against the inside face of the back panel at the vertical midpoint of each woofer chamber (~7.35" from each divider/end wall). They halve the unsupported back panel span. The mid/tweeter chamber gets no batten — lower bass pressure plus acoustic foam lining is sufficient. Install battens before box assembly — you cannot reach in after the sides are on.

Assembly Notes

Build Order (detail)

  1. Lay back panel flat (14.25" × 48.00" × 1/2"). Mark inside face at all batten and divider positions before anything is glued.
  2. Glue & brad back panel battens now — while back panel is still flat and fully accessible. Apply PL Premium bead, press batten, shoot 1" brads from the back face (outside) into each 3/4" batten. Let cure or continue carefully. This is far easier than doing it inside an assembled box. Batten centers: approximately 7.35" above Divider 1 and 7.35" below Divider 2 (vertical midpoint of each woofer chamber).
  3. Glue bottom panel perpendicular at bottom edge. Brad nail every 4–6".
  4. Glue both side panels against back, resting ON TOP of bottom panel. Sides sit 0.50" in from each edge. Before shooting brads: measure both diagonals of the open box (corner to corner). They must be equal. Rack the assembly until diagonals match, then nail.
  5. On the inside face of each side panel, mark both the bottom AND top surface of each divider slot — not just the center:
    • Divider 1: bottom of slot at 14.71", top of slot at 15.21" from inside bottom
    • Divider 2: bottom of slot at 31.29", top of slot at 31.79" from inside bottom
    Mark lines on both side panels with a square. The divider drops into this slot — rest it on the lower line.
  6. Thread woofer cables through divider holes BEFORE gluing dividers.
  7. Dry-fit each divider before gluing: confirm it seats fully flush against the back panel face. Any gap means an air leak between chambers. Check with a finger and straight edge from the open top and front. Fix fit before proceeding.
  8. Glue Divider 1: rest on lower slot line (14.71"). Apply PL, press into place, brad nail through sides into divider edges. Seal cable holes with silicone.
  9. Glue Divider 2: rest on lower slot line (31.29"). Same process.
  10. Glue top panel flush with back. Brad nail every 4–6".
  11. Cure 24 hours minimum.
  12. Seal all internal joints — PL Premium fillet on glue lines, silicone bead on any gaps.
  13. Before installing F-B ties: cut a cardboard template of the baffle hole pattern. Hold it against the box opening and mark where each tie can land with ≥1" clearance from every driver cutout circle. The mid/tweeter chamber gap between driver cutouts is tight — verify positions visually with the template before cutting or placing any ties.
  14. Install 6× F-B ties (3/4" × 3/4" × 4.50"). Glue front face flush with box opening. Brad through back panel from outside (1" brads through 1/2" back panel into tie end grain) to lock alignment during cure.
  15. Stuff woofer chambers with polyfill (~50%).
  16. Line mid/tweeter chamber with 1" acoustic foam.
  17. Route all driver cables.
  18. Verify: all joints sealed, bracing cured, stuffing in place, cables routed, nothing loose inside.
  19. Pre-mark baffle: transfer divider positions from side-panel marks to the front face of the baffle (use a square off the side edges). Mark lines for Divider 1 (at 15.21" from bottom = top surface) and Divider 2 (at 31.79" from bottom = top surface). These are your brad lines — once glue is spread you cannot see divider edges.
  20. Spread PL on all box front edges. Press baffle. Brad nail on pre-marked lines at divider edges, then around perimeter.
  21. Cure 24 hours.
  22. Mount drivers with M4 screws into T-nuts (foam gasket tape under flanges).

Critical Reminders

  • Press T-nuts into baffle back face before gluing baffle to box
  • All internal work must be done before baffle goes on — baffle glue-up is the point of no return
  • Top, bottom & side panels are 4.50" — that is the depth dimension (front-to-back), not the height; they butt against the front face of the back panel
  • Glue back battens to back panel while it is still flat — do not wait until after sides are up
  • Check squareness before brads: equal diagonals on the open box before locking sides in
  • Mark divider SLOT lines (both bottom and top surfaces) on side panels before glue-up
  • Pre-mark divider positions on baffle face before spreading glue — these are blind brad lines
  • Use 1/2" cable holes in dividers (not 3/8")
  • Seal divider cable holes with silicone after threading wires
  • Build one speaker first as a reference before cutting all three
